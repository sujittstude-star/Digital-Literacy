Part A: Case Study

Case Study: UPI and Online Payment Fraud

Overview: UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is a deceptive practice where criminals trick users into authorizing unauthorized transactions. In the context of a college campus, this often manifests as "Reward" or "Refund" scams.

How it Happens (Step-by-Step):

   1)The Bait: The student receives a message on WhatsApp or SMS claiming they have won a contest or are eligible for a scholarship refund.

   2) The Social Engineering: The scammer calls the student, acting as a "bank representative" or "official," and asks them to click a link to "receive" the money.

   3) The Trap: The link opens a payment app (like GPay or PhonePe) and asks the student to enter their UPI PIN.

   4) The Theft: The student mistakenly believes the PIN is to receive funds, but it actually authorizes a debit from their account.

   5) The Disappearance: Once the PIN is entered, the money is instantly transferred to a burner account, and the scammer blocks the student.

Target Audience: College students are frequently targeted due to their high volume of digital transactions and reliance on UPI for daily expenses.

Consequences: Beyond the immediate financial loss, victims often face significant psychological stress, a loss of trust in digital systems, and potential difficulty in recovering funds if the theft isn't reported immediately.
